Cases Where People Misinterpret Data

When intuition fails and the numbers lead us astray.

Data should be objective, but the way we interpret it often isn't. Cognitive biases and statistical paradoxes can cause even the most expert analysts to draw the wrong conclusions from a dataset.

The Correlation vs. Causation Trap

One of the most common mistakes is assuming that because two metrics move together, one must cause the other. For example, ice cream sales and shark attacks both increase during the summer, but ice cream doesn't cause shark attacks. The hidden variable here is temperature.

Simpson's Paradox

Simpson’s Paradox occurs when a trend appears in different groups of data but disappears or reverses when these groups are combined. This can lead to misleading conclusions in everything from hospital survival rates to marketing campaign results.

The Survivorship Bias

We often focus on the "winners" and ignore the much larger group of "losers" that we don't see. In business analytics, this often means studying successful companies while ignoring the data from those that failed, leading to a skewed understanding of what truly drives success.
