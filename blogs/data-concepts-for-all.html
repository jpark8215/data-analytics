Explaining Data Concepts to Non-Experts

Making the complex simple for better decision making.

Data science is full of jargon, p-values, over-fitting, random forests, neural networks. To the non-expert, this can feel like an impenetrable wall. However, the most successful data teams are those that can translate these concepts into plain language.

What is "Significance" Anyway?

In data, "statistically significant" doesn't necessarily mean "important." It simply means that whatever you're seeing isn't likely just a coincidence. Explaining this can prevent business leaders from overreacting to small but statistically sound shifts in information.

Algorithms are just Recipes

At its heart, an algorithm is just a set of instructions, much like a recipe in a cookbook. Explaining them this way removes the "black box" mystery and builds trust with stakeholders who need to understand how conclusions are being reached.

The Power of Visualization

A good chart should tell a story instantly. If you have to spend 10 minutes explaining a visualization, it’s not working. Simple, clean, and direct visualizations are often much more effective than complex, artistic ones for non-expert audiences.
