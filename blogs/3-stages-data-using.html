The 3 Stages Every Company Goes Through on the Way to Actually Using Data

A maturity model with honest commentary on what each phase really looks like.

Nearly every company today aims to be "data-driven." However, the path from spreadsheets to strategic enablement isn't linear, nor is it easy. It's often fraught with misaligned expectations, organizational resistance, and the realization that software alone doesn't solve culture problems. Here is an honest, practical look at the three foundational stages of data maturity.

Stage 1: Starting with Data (The "Wild West" Phase)

The Reality: In this early adoption stage, data tasks are mostly reactive. Leadership asks ad hoc questions, and whoever knows SQL or Advanced Excel cobbles together an answer. The focus is simply on accessing the data and getting "quick wins" before anyone questions the ROI of the software stack.

The Honest Commentary: Data engineers in this stage are generalists. They do the plumbing, the analyzing, and the reporting. There are rarely formalized processes, meaning technical debt accrues immediately. Organizations often mistake buying a business intelligence tool for having a data strategy. Prioritizing simple, off-the-shelf tools instead of overly complex infrastructure is key here.

Stage 2: Scaling with Data (The "Process and Pipeline" Phase)

The Reality: At this stage, the pain of manual reporting has become unbearable. The organization begins to consolidate data sources into centralized data warehouses or lakes. Processes like ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) or ELT emerge to formalize data flows. Adoption of systematic DataOps and DevOps practices begins.

The Honest Commentary: This is arguably the hardest phase. Scaling requires deep transitions such as formalizing role specializations (e.g., dedicated Data Engineers vs. Data Analysts). It is also the phase where managers discover that breaking down departmental data silos is mostly a political struggle, not a technical one. Success in this stage requires persistent sponsorship from the top to force departments to share data.

Stage 3: Leading with Data (The "Strategic Asset" Phase)

The Reality: In the final, most mature stage, data is deeply embedded in the organization's culture and decision-making apparatus. Data pipelines are scalable and automated. Stakeholders engage with self-service analytics safely. Machine learning models may be actively driving product features or internal efficiencies.

The Honest Commentary: Very few companies actually maintain this phase consistently across all departments. The hallmark of Stage 3 isn't just having the best predictive models; it’s the cultural humility to allow data to override established hierarchy and "gut feel." A true Stage 3 company continuously iterates, managing its data as a first-class product with clear SLAs and a strong focus on data literacy across non-technical teams.

Conclusion: Remember, the journey through these stages isn't a waterfall project. It's an iterative progression. Avoid over-engineering solutions before the business needs them, and remember that tools are only 20% of the battle, the rest is culture, incentives, and decision processes.
