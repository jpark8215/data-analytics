# Custom Blog Writing Style

When writing blog posts or content for this workspace, strictly adhere to the following writing style and rules:

1. **Tone and Voice**: 
   - **Academic & Analytical**: Maintain a formal, objective, and contemplative tone. Avoid overly casual language, exclamation marks, or colloquialisms. 
   - **Balanced Perspective**: Present well-rounded arguments that weigh both positive achievements and negative shortcomings equally.

2. **Structure and Flow**:
   - **Topic Sentences**: Start paragraphs with clear topic sentences that state the core idea (e.g., "The function of organizing consists of...").
   - **Transitions**: Use formal transitional adverbs to connect thoughts smoothly (e.g., *Furthermore*, *Additionally*, *However*, *Even though*, *Yet*).
   - **Sentence Length**: Use a mix of medium to long, structurally sound sentences.

3. **Vocabulary**:
   - Use elevated and precise language (e.g., "unarguably successful," "immovable," "shamefully missed out," "fruitful results," "repressing potential").

4. **Evidence-Based Arguments**:
   - Back up claims with concrete facts, specific metrics, historical context, and exact figures (e.g., "revenue rose from $25 billion to $70 billion," "7 percent stock jump"). 
   - **Cite Authoritative Sources**: Frequently cite specific studies, academic papers, universities, and authoritative figures by name and title (e.g., "Christine Jolls, professor of Law...", "A survey conducted by Jonathan Webb..."). Do not make vague generalizations.

5. **Strict Constraints**:
   - **NO em dashes**. Do not use "—" anywhere in the text.
   - **No text in images**: Any generated hero images must be abstract and contain absolutely no text, words, labels, or numbers.
